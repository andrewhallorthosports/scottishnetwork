Nationwide Cohort Study · Trauma

The Epidemiology and Outcomes of Periprosthetic Femur Fractures: The Scottish National Audit of Periprosthetic Femur Fractures (SNAP Femur)

Kennedy MJ, Clement ND, Farrow L, Kennedy IW, Harding T, SCOTnet SNAP Femur Group, Blacklock C, Penfold RS, Clarke JV, Duckworth AD, MacLullich AMJ, Walmsley PJ, Hall AJ

The Bone & Joint Journal · 2026;108-B(5):707–715 · doi:10.1302/0301-620X.108B5.BJJ-2025-0979.R1

A nationwide retrospective cohort study of 328 periprosthetic femur fractures managed across 16 Scottish centres in 2019. One-year mortality was 20.8% (SMR 2.74). Independent mortality predictors included interprosthetic fracture (aHR 2.65), male sex (aHR 1.76), older age, and higher pre-injury frailty. Management strategy was not independently associated with mortality after adjustment.

Scoping Review · Metabolic & Joint Health

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists and Weight-Loss Strategies for Individuals with Obesity and Hip or Knee Osteoarthritis

McCann C, Clement N, Murray A, Kearney R, Madigan S, Gee C, Walmsley P, Morrison S, Chopra S, Hall A

British Journal of Sports Medicine · Epub ahead of print, 2026 · doi:10.1136/bjsports-2025-111225

A PRISMA-ScR scoping review of 199 studies examining weight-loss strategies in adults with obesity and hip or knee osteoarthritis, with focus on the emerging role of GLP-1 receptor agonists. Of 36 studies directly assessing GLP-1-RAs, 14 reported original data. Evidence is strongest for knee OA; hip OA data remain limited. GLP-1-RAs are recommended as adjuncts within supervised, multimodal musculoskeletal care.

Scoping Review · Patient & Public Perception

Perceptions of Robot-Assisted Arthroplasty Among Patients and the Public

Morrison SR, Clement ND, Clarke JV, Gee C, Chopra S, Hall AJ

Bone & Joint Open · 2026;7(6):733–743 · doi:10.1302/2633-1462.76.BJO-2026-0007.R1

A PRISMA-ScR scoping review of 16 studies (3,957 participants) exploring patient and public perceptions of robot-assisted surgery (RAS) in arthroplasty. Four themes emerged: interest, understanding, concerns, and influences on preference. Patients showed high interest but commonly overestimated RAS benefits and prevalence of use, with trust in the operating surgeon remaining a stronger influence than the technology itself.

About the Network

Clinical research, built across Scotland

SCOTTISH Network links three Scottish institutions across national hospital, academic and clinical settings. The network was established to conduct collaborative, high-quality research in orthopaedic surgery and musculoskeletal health, addressing questions that require population-scale data and multi-site expertise.

Research outputs are designed to inform clinical practice, national audit, and health policy across Scotland and beyond.

Epidemiology
National-level cohort studies drawing on Scottish health data to establish the burden and outcomes of orthopaedic conditions.
Clinical Outcomes
Rigorous evaluation of surgical and non-surgical interventions, with mortality, function and patient-reported outcomes as primary endpoints.
Musculoskeletal Health
Translational and systematic research into metabolic, pharmacological and lifestyle determinants of joint health and surgical outcomes.
Collaborative Science
Trainee-led and consultant-supervised research through SCOTnet and national audit infrastructure, with multi-disciplinary authorship.
